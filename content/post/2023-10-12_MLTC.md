We continue to contribute to the [MUM-PREDICT](https://mumpredict.org/) and [OPTIMAL](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/research/applied-health/research/optimal/index.aspx) projects over the last six months including:

- [Artificial Intelligence for Multiple Long-term conditions (AIM): A consensus statement from the NIHR AIM consortia](https://openresearch.nihr.ac.uk/documents/3-21)

- [Maternal and child outcomes for pregnant women with pre-existing multiple long-term conditions: protocol for an observational study in the UK](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/13/2/e068718.abstract)

- [Polypharmacy during pregnancy and associated risk factors: a retrospective analysis of 577 medication exposures among 1.5 million pregnancies in the UK, 2000-2019](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12916-022-02722-5)

- [The development of a core outcome set for studies of pregnant women with multimorbidity](https://bmcmedicine.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12916-023-03013-3)

- [Detection and evaluation of signals associated with exposure to individual and combination of medications in pregnancy: a signal detection study protocol](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/13/10/e073162.abstract)
